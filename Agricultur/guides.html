No-Till Wheat: Precision Planting
Cereals • Spring • Precision

Practical steps to implement no-till wheat using precision seeding and nutrient management.

Step-by-step
  1. Field assessment: measure soil compaction and residue cover.
  2. Calibrate planter for seed depth and spacing per seed rate chart.
  3. Apply starter fertilizer near seed row per soil test recommendations.
  4. Monitor emergence and adjust seeding maps with yield sensors.
  5. Scout regularly for early pests and diseases.
Tips & Best Practices
  • Use cover crops to maintain soil structure between seasons.
  • Record GPS-based planting data to refine future maps.
  • Prioritize soil tests annually for precise nutrient inputs.
Drip Irrigation for Tomatoes
Vegetables • Summer • Irrigation

Efficient water delivery and scheduling to improve yield and fruit quality.

Step-by-step
  1. Design layout: calculate flow and spacing for emitters per bed length.
  2. Install pressure regulator and filtration to prevent clogging.
  3. Lay mainline and sublines; place emitters near root zones.
  4. Schedule irrigation based on evapotranspiration (ET) and crop stage.
  5. Flush lines monthly and check uniformity across rows.
Tips & Best Practices
  • Use mulches to reduce evaporation and maintain moisture.
  • Monitor soil moisture with probes to avoid over-watering.
  • Replace clogged emitters promptly and keep spare parts.
Organic Mango Orchard Management
Fruits • Rainy • Organic

Integrated organic practices for disease control and soil health in orchards.

Step-by-step
  1. Sanitation: remove and destroy diseased fruit and prunings.
  2. Promote beneficials via flowering strips and habitat plants.
  3. Apply approved organic fungicides and monitor regularly.
  4. Manage waterlogging with raised beds or drainage.
  5. Use compost and green manures to boost soil biology.
Tips & Best Practices
  • Time interventions to weather forecasts to reduce disease pressure.
  • Keep detailed records for certification and improvement.
Conservation Practices for Pulses
Legumes • Winter • Conservation

Soil moisture conservation and residue management to improve pulse yields.

Step-by-step
  1. Retain crop residues and apply mulches after harvest.
  2. Use contour farming or ridges to reduce runoff.
  3. Optimize sowing date for soil moisture availability.
  4. Use inoculated seed to boost nitrogen fixation.
  5. Rotate with non-host crops to manage diseases.
Tips & Best Practices
  • Test soil for pH and micro-nutrients before planting.
  • Integrate livestock grazing carefully to preserve residues.
Integrated Pest Management (IPM) for Leafy Greens
Vegetables • Spring • Organic

Multi-layered pest control combining cultural, biological and selective inputs.

Step-by-step
  1. Set up traps and monitoring to quantify pest thresholds.
  2. Use row covers and crop rotation for cultural control.
  3. Release or conserve natural enemies where appropriate.
  4. If needed, apply low-toxicity products at recommended timings.
  5. Record pest events and treatments for continuous improvement.
Tips & Best Practices
  • Prioritize preventive measures and habitat for predators.
  • Avoid broad-spectrum sprays that disrupt beneficial insects.
Nutrient Management for Maize
Cereals • Summer • Precision

Site-specific nutrient planning to maximize efficiency and reduce losses.

Step-by-step
  1. Conduct grid soil sampling and analyze for macro and micro nutrients.
  2. Create variable-rate maps based on yield and soil data.
  3. Split fertilizer applications: base + sidedress at V6-V8 stages.
  4. Use inhibitors where applicable to reduce nitrogen loss.
  5. Evaluate tissue tests during critical growth stages.
Tips & Best Practices
  • Match nutrient form and placement to soil type.
  • Use on-farm demonstrations to validate map recommendations.
