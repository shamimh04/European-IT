Interviews Archive

Recorded interviews of individuals who played significant roles or were affected by the Liberation War of Bangladesh

Voices from 1971

These interviews preserve personal testimonies, life stories and historical experiences of the Liberation War.

Abdul Karim
Freedom Fighter

Biography: Abdul Karim was a member of the Mukti Bahini who actively participated in guerrilla warfare during the Liberation War of 1971.

Testimony Summary: He describes battlefield experiences, hardships, sacrifices, and the motivation that drove fighters to risk their lives for independence.

Rashida Begum
Eyewitness

Biography: Rashida Begum was a civilian living in a border area and witnessed the impact of the war on ordinary people.

Testimony Summary: She shares memories of displacement, loss of family members, and the refugee crisis during the war.

Dr. Mahbub Rahman
Historian

Biography: Dr. Mahbub Rahman is a historian specializing in the Liberation War of Bangladesh.

Testimony Summary: He explains the political background, international reactions, and long-term significance of the war.

Salma Khatun
Women’s Role

Biography: Salma Khatun supported freedom fighters by providing shelter, food, and medical assistance.

Testimony Summary: She highlights the role of women and the suffering they endured during the Liberation War.
